An **apostille** from India’s **Ministry of External Affairs (MEA)** validates your birth certificate for **use in any Hague Convention country**.  
VerifiedApostille assists Indians living abroad, NRIs, and international clients to complete this process quickly and securely.

---

## Sticker vs e-Apostille (QR)

- **Sticker Apostille (physical):** MEA sticker applied directly on the document — widely accepted for visas, migration, or legal submissions.  
- **e-Apostille (QR):** Digital MEA apostille with QR verification — secure, shareable, and accepted by most countries.

## Documents Required

- **Birth certificate** (scan or original, as advised)  
- **Passport** (first & last page) of the individual named  
- **Parent/guardian ID** (for minors or overseas applications)  
- **Affidavit/correction proof** if any mismatch in name/date

**Accepted certificate types:**

- Municipal / corporation / panchayat **originals**  
- **Reissued copies** from municipal records  
- **Hospital-issued** certificates endorsed by municipal authorities  
- **Notarized true copies** when accepted by destination country

---

## Step-by-Step Process

1. **Share a scan** for eligibility review (Sticker vs e-Apostille).  
2. **Pickup or courier your document** — we verify and prepare.  
3. **Notary (if needed)**.  
4. **SDM or State Home Department attestation**.  
5. **MEA Apostille** — Sticker or e-Apostille (QR).  
6. **Digital confirmation** — scanned copy + verification link.  
7. **Courier dispatch** — worldwide delivery with tracking.

## Processing Time

Most apostilles complete in **2-3 working days** once the document is in process.  
State or reissue steps may extend the timeline — we share your confirmed schedule upfront.

## Original vs Copy

- **Embassies / Visa authorities** often require **Sticker apostille** on the **original**.  
- Many **universities / HR agencies** accept **e-Apostille (QR)** on notarized copies.  
- We always follow your **recipient’s written requirement** to avoid rejection.
